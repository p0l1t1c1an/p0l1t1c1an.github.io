Why I Chose To Study Cybersecurity

I would say that my vast interest in the security field comes from my interest in well computers, obviously. I still remember being a youngin' like 6-7 and my family giving me their old phones. I would tear those open and look at all the components, then try to put them back together with a 99% failure rate. It wasn't until middle school that I started getting into programming with vex controllers in C++. And in high school, I found interests lasting to this day with using FOSS and Unix-like OSes or low level programming in C (and now Rust). I think cybersecurity is a way for me to learn more about these systems that I took for granted and couldn't even start to explain growing up.

On top of this, my family has been victims of identity theft. So, I have experienced and seen the issues people face with lack of security either due to personal failure or an institution's failure. I really just want to minimize these things like identity theft and credit card fraud happening for everyone. Protecting their personal information. So in a way, I want to do my part to protect people from what I have seen happen with my own family when I was younger.

Finally, my interest in politics from high school wraps up why I am studying security nicely. That is politics seems coincide with the same lower level ideas as security. Those being topics like psychology, criminology, economics, and history making these two interests pair together well. In all honesty, I would likely be studying computer engineering or science instead of security (with a politics minor) if not for my attraction to politics. My focus being shifted away from security if I didn't find politics enjoyable.
