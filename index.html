Welcome to my website!

Hello there! I'm Jacob Boicken. I'm a Cybersecurity Engineering junior at Iowa State University with a minor in Political Science. I am hoping to have a job in the cybersecurity field in the future. I lean more on the defensive (blue) team side, but I would love work and try the many fields of security.

In my free time, I tend to work on my homeserver running FreeBSD, code on a few Rust utility programs, keep up to date on current events in both the cyber and political worlds, and read books on topics like security, psychology, and politics.

Discovering My Interests

When I was child, I would take my family's old laptops and cell phones and dissect them trying to learn more about them. From there, I got interested in circuits and started working with Vex robotics, Arduinos, and Raspberry Pis learning to program in C++ and Python. Then on, I discovered more interesting things like Unix operating systems and low level programming in C. To this day, I am still finding new things to learn and take interest in from low level ideas of networking and wireless communications to high level ideas of web development and setting my own NAS.

Why Cybersecurity

I'm constantly asked why I chose cybersecurity versus going into computer engineering or computer science. Well, it's mainly because security is far more interesting and spreads out to other fields than simply computers. While it does focus on computers in the more techinical aspects, it also incorporates many other ideas and fields. From psychology and criminology to finance and policy, each of these ideas are vital to understand for security operations to occur. And all of them are incredibly interesting to learn about and gain experience in.

What fields do I want to go into

As I've shown above, I like to live by the ideas of being a "Jack of all trades, master of none". By this, I mean I want to generalize instead of specializing. That way I can experience and be knowledgeable in every field instead of just one or two. Though, there are some general fields I am deeply interested in learning about more:

  • Wireless and Network Security
  • Cloud Security
  • Security Auditing
  • Operating System Security
  • Cybersecurity Policy and Law
